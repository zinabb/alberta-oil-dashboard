Key Insights

Simple Linear Regression Forecast:
The linear regression model reflects the long-term upward trajectory in Alberta’s oil production observed across the historical dataset. By extending this structural trend forward, the model projects a continued gradual increase into 2026, assuming historical growth drivers remain broadly intact. While useful for understanding directional momentum, this approach does not account for short-term volatility, cyclical behavior, or external shocks, and should therefore be interpreted as a baseline trend indicator rather than a precise forecast.

ARIMA Forecast:
The ARIMA model produces a more cautious outlook, anchoring its projection in recent production dynamics rather than long-run growth. The forecast suggests stabilization to slightly declining output, with confidence intervals widening materially over time. This expansion highlights increasing uncertainty at longer horizons, indicating that near-term projections are more reliable than estimates further into the future. The model emphasizes sensitivity to recent fluctuations rather than structural expansion.

Strategic Takeaway:
The contrast between the two models illustrates how forecast outcomes depend heavily on modeling assumptions. Linear regression highlights long-term structural growth, while ARIMA emphasizes recent behavior and uncertainty. Together, they frame a realistic range of potential outcomes, reinforcing the importance of scenario-based planning rather than reliance on a single forecast when assessing Alberta’s future oil production.
