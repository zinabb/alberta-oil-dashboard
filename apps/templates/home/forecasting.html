Key Insights

Simple Linear Regression Forecast: This model presents a steady upward projection of Alberta's oil production, consistent with the long-term expansion observed since the early 2000s. It assumes a stable rate of growth without accounting for market volatility or policy changes—making it suitable for long-term trend analysis, but less reliable for capturing short-term disruptions or economic shifts.

ARIMA Forecast: In contrast, the ARIMA model introduces a more statistically rigorous outlook by incorporating time-based patterns and seasonality. It forecasts a relatively flat output with a widening confidence interval into 2026, highlighting uncertainty due to recent market fluctuations or data limitations. This model is better equipped to detect production cycles, inflection points, or abrupt shifts in trend, making it valuable for short- to medium-term planning.

Strategic Takeaway: The contrasting forecasts demonstrate Alberta’s production resilience while emphasizing the importance of selecting forecasting tools that balance trend clarity with statistical sensitivity. Integrating both methods can enhance foresight in navigating demand shocks, regulatory interventions, or infrastructure constraints.
