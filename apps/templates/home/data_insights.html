Key Insights

The combined visual analyses provide insight into both asset-level production behavior and system-level production relationships within Alberta’s oil sector.

Exponential Decline Curve:
The exponential decline curve illustrates the typical production lifecycle of an oil well, characterized by a steep initial decline followed by a progressively slower rate of decay. This pattern reflects natural reservoir depletion and declining pressure over time. While the curve is illustrative rather than calibrated to a specific asset, it highlights a fundamental industry reality, sustaining aggregate production requires continuous reinvestment through new drilling, enhanced recovery techniques, or operational optimization, as legacy wells alone cannot maintain output indefinitely.

Correlation Matrix (All Time):
Across the full historical dataset, Total Oil Production exhibits an almost perfect correlation with Non-Conventional Oil production, indicating that long-term changes in Alberta’s aggregate output have been driven primarily by non-conventional sources. Conventional Oil shows a moderate positive correlation with total production, suggesting a supporting but less dominant role in shaping long-run production trends. The weaker relationship between conventional and non-conventional production implies that these streams have historically followed distinct development paths rather than moving in lockstep.

Correlation Matrix (Last 5 Years):
When focusing on the most recent five years, correlations strengthen across all production categories. The relationship between Conventional and Non-Conventional Oil production increases markedly, while both show strong correlations with total production. This shift suggests that short-term fluctuations in Alberta’s output are increasingly influenced by both production streams, reflecting more integrated operational dynamics in the near term compared to the longer historical period.

Strategic Takeaway:
Taken together, these visuals highlight the contrast between natural decline at the well level and structural dependence on non-conventional production at the provincial level. While non-conventional oil underpins Alberta’s long-term production capacity, conventional output plays a more meaningful role in short-term variability. This underscores the importance of balanced investment strategies that manage decline, volatility, and long-term supply sustainability simultaneously.
